# Cross-Border Healthcare Policy: Mainland Patients in Hong Kong

## Overview

This guide covers policies and practical considerations for Mainland Chinese patients seeking cancer treatment in Hong Kong. It also references relevant policies for Hong Kong residents seeking care on the Mainland.

## Legal Framework

### Entry Requirements for Medical Treatment

**For Mainland Residents**:

1. **Travel Document**: Mainland Travel Permit for Hong Kong and Macau (往来港澳通行证)
2. **Entry Permit**: Multiple-entry endorsement for medical visits
3. **Visa Duration**: Typically 7 days initially; extendable at Immigration Department

### Important Note

As of the current policy framework, Hong Kong does not have a formal medical visa program specifically for patients. However:

- Patients can enter as tourists and receive outpatient services
- Inpatient treatment requires admission arrangement with hospital
- Extended stays for treatment require proper documentation

## Current Policy Environment

### Historical Context: Hong Kong → Mainland Flow

Most cross-border healthcare policy discussion in Hong Kong has focused on **Hong Kong residents seeking care in Mainland China**, particularly in the Greater Bay Area (GBA). This includes:

- Elderly Health Care Voucher (EHCV) GBA Pilot Scheme
- Hospital Authority GBA Patient Support Scheme
- eHealth cross-boundary health record sharing

### Mainland → Hong Kong Considerations

**For Mainland patients seeking treatment in Hong Kong:**

1. **No formal barriers** - Mainland residents can access Hong Kong private healthcare
2. **Full cost payment** - Not eligible for public hospital subsidies
3. **Language considerations** - Mandarin-speaking staff may be limited in some private hospitals
4. **Medical records transfer** - Coordination between mainland and HK medical systems

## Practical Pathways for Mainland Cancer Patients

### Pathway 1: Direct Private Hospital Referral

**Steps**:

1. Obtain referral from mainland oncologist
2. Contact Hong Kong private hospital international patient services
3. Submit medical records for assessment
4. Arrange appointment and travel
5. Pay deposit and receive treatment

**Required Documents**:
- Valid travel permit (往来港澳通行证)
- Medical records (in Chinese or with translation)
- Pathology reports
- Imaging films/CDs
- Treatment plan from mainland physician

**Hospitals with International Patient Services**:
- 养和医院 (HKSH) - International Patient Center
- 香港港安医院–荃湾
- Other private hospitals with international patient programs

### Pathway 2: Clinical Trial Participation

**Considerations**:

- Some HK hospitals conduct clinical trials
- Eligibility may require HK residency or specific visa categories
- Limited availability; competitive enrollment

**Resources**:
- HK Clinical Research Ethics Committee approvals
- Hospital-by-hospital clinical trial databases

### Pathway 3: Drug Access Programs

**Mechanisms**:

1. **Personal Import**: Patients can import drugs for personal use
   - Requires prescription from registered physician
   - Customs declaration required
   - Quantity limits apply

2. **Hospital Procurement**: Private hospitals can procure unapproved drugs
   - For in-patient use under physician supervision
   - Case-by-case basis

## Cost Considerations

### Payment Requirements

| Hospital Type | Payment Expectation |
|---------------|---------------------|
| Private Hospitals | Deposit required at admission; balance due before discharge |
| Deposit Range | HK$10,000 - HK$50,000 depending on procedure |
| Settlement Currency | HKD; some accept CNY or have conversion |

### Insurance Considerations

1. **Mainland Health Insurance**
   - Generally NOT valid in Hong Kong
   - Some commercial insurance may have HK coverage

2. **Travel Insurance**
   - Verify HK coverage for pre-existing conditions
   - Cancer treatment typically excluded or limited

3. **International Health Insurance**
   - Check HK network hospitals
   - Pre-authorization usually required

## Drug Import Regulations

### Personal Import Allowance

**Rules**:
- Drugs for personal use can be imported without separate approval
- Must accompany traveler with prescription documentation
- Quantity: typically 3-month supply maximum

**For Targeted Therapy Drugs**:
- Maintain original packaging
- Carry prescription or letter from physician
- Declare at customs if asked

### Key Drugs Commonly Sought

| Drug Category | Examples | Consideration |
|---------------|----------|---------------|
| PD-1 Inhibitors | Pembrolizumab, Nivolumab | Registered in HK; available |
| EGFR TKIs | Osimertinib, Aumolertinib, Furmonertinib | Some China-only; import options |
| Other Targeted | Various | Case-by-case |

## Greater Bay Area (GBA) Developments

### Relevant GBA Policies (Primarily HK Residents)

Although focused on HK residents, these initiatives demonstrate integration:

1. **EHCV GBA Pilot Scheme** (长者医疗券大湾区试点计划)
   - Covers residents aged 65+
   - Expanded to 21 service points across GBA (as of August 2025)
   - Includes outpatient services only

2. **HA GBA Patient Support Scheme** (医院管理局大湾区病人支援计划)
   - Follow-up consultations at designated GBA hospitals
   - Over 4,400 patients participated as of mid-2024

3. **eHealth Cross-boundary Functions** (eHealth跨境界别功能)
   - Implemented July 2024
   - Allows HK residents to access electronic health records in designated Mainland institutions
   - Self-deposit Mainland medical records into personal eHealth folder

### Implications for Mainland → HK Patients

While these programs are designed for HK residents, they reflect improving cross-boundary healthcare infrastructure that may eventually benefit bidirectional patient flow.

## Medical Records Coordination

### Transferring Records from Mainland to HK

**Recommended Documents**:
1. Complete medical history
2. Pathology reports (original or certified copy)
3. Imaging studies (DICOM format preferred)
4. Treatment summaries
5. Genetic/molecular testing results
6. Surgical notes if applicable

**Translation Considerations**:
- Some HK hospitals accept Chinese records
- English translation may be required for insurance purposes
- Professional medical translation services available

### Follow-up Care Coordination

**Options**:
1. **Parallel Care**: Treatment in HK with mainland physician monitoring
2. **Transfer**: Complete transition to HK care
3. **Interim Care**: HK treatment with planned return to Mainland

## Regulatory Considerations

### Professional Practice

- Hong Kong doctors must be licensed by Medical Council of Hong Kong
- Second opinions from HK specialists are permissible
- Telemedicine from HK to Mainland patients may have limitations

### Drug Regulation

- All drugs administered in HK must be registered with Drug Office (except personal import)
- Prescription requirements apply
- Clinical trial drugs have separate oversight

### Practical Tips

1. **Before Traveling**:
   - Confirm appointment with hospital
   - Obtain comprehensive medical records
   - Verify accepted payment methods
   - Arrange accommodation near hospital

2. **At Immigration**:
   - State purpose as medical treatment if asked
   - Carry appointment confirmation letter
   - Have proof of financial means

3. **During Treatment**:
   - Keep all receipts and medical reports
   - Request English summaries for future mainland care
   - Clarify follow-up schedule before returning
